Blockchain

      A blockchain is a growing list of records, called blocks, which are linked using cryptography.

      Due to its design, blockchain is very resistant to modification of these records without the approval of everyone on its peer-to-peer network.

      The data in any given block cannot be altered retroactively without alteration of all subsequent blocks, which requires consensus of the network majority.

      Blockchain was invented by a person using the name Satoshi Nakamoto in 2008 to serve as the public transaction ledger of the cryptocurrency Bitcoin.

      The security and openness blockchain provides makes it perfect for use inn decentralised cryptocurrency.

      Blockchain has also found use within the financial services industry, by implementing distributed ledgers for use in banking.

Autonomous Things (Robots)

      These are the technological developments that are expected to bring computers into the physical environment as autonomous entities without human direction.

      These technologies all make extensive use of machine learning and artificial intelligence.

      Examples of future implementations of these technologies would be self-driving cars.

      Due to safety concerns of the potential negative impact of the new technology, they all require unprecedented levels of safety and security.

      Another potential use of these technologies home robotics, caring for both the elderly and the young.


Immersive Experiences

      Immersive experience is an illusory environment that completely surrounds you such that you feel that you are inside it and part of it.

      The most common form of this would be the use of virtual reality headsets, such as the HTC Vive and Oculus Rift.

      Another promising use of immersive experience is in psychiatric care, with medical professional able to visualize and better understand psychotic states of their patients

      Architects and building engineers are incorporating immersive experiences into their designs to better visualize the scale and depth of their buildings.

      One issue that solutions are being developed for is the prevalency of users who experience simulation sickness during use of immersive experience.
